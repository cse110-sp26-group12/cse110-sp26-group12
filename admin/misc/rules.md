# Team Charter: Group Values & Operating Protocols

This document serves as our "Source of Truth" for team culture, expectations, and conflict resolution. We will discuss and finalize our answers to these questions during our kickoff meeting.

---

## I. Mission & Core Values

1. **The Team Mission:** In one sentence, what is our primary goal for this project? (e.g., academic excellence, building a portfolio piece, or simply efficient completion?)

  Getting experience, practicing technical skills, having new projects on Github page, making new friends, learn how to work in a team. 

3. **Core Values:** What are the 3-5 non-negotiable principles we will uphold? (e.g., "Technical Rigor," "Radical Transparency," "Respect for Time.")

Timely response, productivity, accountability, respect for time.

---

## II. Communication & Responsiveness

1. **The Response Window:** What is the maximum time a member can wait before responding to a tag or direct question in Slack/Discord? Does this change on weekends or near deadlines?

8 hrs, and within the same day as possible

3. **Source of Truth:** Where do final decisions live? If a technical decision is made in a DM, how is it communicated to the rest of the group to ensure no one is working on outdated info

Everyone agrees to turn on slack notifications, and makes sure to check slack server at least once per day. In case if any important decisions are made, members are required to make it public in #general or for team leads, #announcements

In addition, all important technical advancements will be reported in README.md in the root directory. 

In case of any discrptancy between slack and README.md, README.md is the sole source of truth.


4. **Meeting Etiquette:** What are the expectations for attendance, cameras (if remote), and preparation? What is the protocol if a member absolutely cannot make a meeting? What steps do a typical meeting include?

Members need to discuss in advance on why they cannot make it to the meeting in #general. Members agree that cameras will stay on during meeting and carry out instructions made in #announcements by the team leads in advance. 

Typical meetings include the following steps: 1. members in the meeting will take turns to describe what they have done in the previous week and what they plan to do next week. 2. Team leads give new updates on the direction of the projects. 3. The rest of the meeting will be dedicated towards carrying out any team activities Powell releases.

## III. Roles, Accountabilities & Workflow

1. **Division of Labor:** How do we ensure that "non-coding" tasks (documentation, admin, branding) are distributed fairly so the workload doesn't fall solely on the Leads?

Team leads will split their work among team members evenly to carry out tasks. 

3. **Technical Ownership:** When a member is assigned a module, do they have total creative control, or is there a required "Design Review" before they start coding?

  Team leads will decide on the general direction each team member works for the project.

5. **Code Quality & PRs:** What is our Pull Request (PR) policy? Who is allowed to merge to `main`? What is the minimum number of reviewers required for a technical change?

Members should submit a pull request to merge to main and if developers want to submit a PR then they must be approved by a lead.

6. **The "Definition of Done":** When is a task actually "finished"? (e.g., Code written? Tests passed? Documentation updated in the repo?)

Depends on the task. For larger tasks like the project, all documentations must be maintained and for smaller projects it is considered finished when reviewed by a team lead.

For a PR, it is "finished" when the team lead approves it.

Team leads will first send them a warning message, and if the warning isgnored until the end of the current (day) or next (night) business hours (9am - 5pm), the situation is escalated

## V. Conflict Resolution & Psychological Safety

1. **Technical Disagreements:** When two members disagree on a technical implementation (e.g., architecture, language choice, or tool), how is the tie broken?

If two member disagree, they should imform the team leads to make a decision. If the team leads disagree then they will create a vote in #announcements to decide the winner

2. **Interpersonal Conflict:** If tension arises between two members, what is our "Safe Space" protocol for resolving it without it spilling into the general group chat?

If things are getting personal between two members, they should message the team leads about the conflict and they will do something about it

3. **Mistake Protocol:** How do we handle it when someone accidentally "breaks the build" or misses a requirement? How do we focus on the "fix" rather than the "blame"?

Everyone makes misakes but we encourage good record-keeping, i.e. commit early commit often. If we make any mistakes we should learn from it avoid making that misake again.
