# Team Charter: Group Values & Operating Protocols

This document serves as our "Source of Truth" for team culture, expectations, and conflict resolution. We will discuss and finalize our answers to these questions during our kickoff meeting.

---

## I. Mission & Core Values

1. **The Team Mission:** In one sentence, what is our primary goal for this project? (e.g., academic excellence, building a portfolio piece, or simply efficient completion?)

2. **Core Values:** What are the 3-5 non-negotiable principles we will uphold? (e.g., "Technical Rigor," "Radical Transparency," "Respect for Time.")


---

## II. Communication & Responsiveness

1. **The Response Window:** What is the maximum time a member can wait before responding to a tag or direct question in Slack/Discord? Does this change on weekends or near deadlines?

2. **Source of Truth:** Where do final decisions live? If a technical decision is made in a DM, how is it communicated to the rest of the group to ensure no one is working on outdated info?

3. **Meeting Etiquette:** What are the expectations for attendance, cameras (if remote), and preparation? What is the protocol if a member absolutely cannot make a meeting? What steps do a typical meeting include?

---

## III. Roles, Accountabilities & Workflow

1. **Division of Labor:** How do we ensure that "non-coding" tasks (documentation, admin, branding) are distributed fairly so the workload doesn't fall solely on the Leads?

2. **Technical Ownership:** When a member is assigned a module, do they have total creative control, or is there a required "Design Review" before they start coding?

3. **Code Quality & PRs:** What is our Pull Request (PR) policy? Who is allowed to merge to `main`? What is the minimum number of reviewers required for a technical change?

4. **The "Definition of Done":** When is a task actually "finished"? (e.g., Code written? Tests passed? Documentation updated in the repo?)

---

## IV. The "Free-Rider" & Performance Protocol

1. **Internal Deadlines:** How far in advance of the Canvas/TA deadline should internal drafts be completed to allow for group review?

2. **Addressing Non-Responsiveness:** If a member misses a response window or a meeting without notice, what is the immediate Step 1? (e.g., A private check-in from a Lead? A group notification?)

3. **Escalation Path:** If a member repeatedly fails to deliver or communicate, what process before the issue is officially escalated to Jared (the TA)?



---

## V. Conflict Resolution & Psychological Safety

1. **Technical Disagreements:** When two members disagree on a technical implementation (e.g., architecture, language choice, or tool), how is the tie broken?

2. **Interpersonal Conflict:** If tension arises between two members, what is our "Safe Space" protocol for resolving it without it spilling into the general group chat?

3. **Mistake Protocol:** How do we handle it when someone accidentally "breaks the build" or misses a requirement? How do we focus on the "fix" rather than the "blame"?

---

## VI. Team Logistics



1. **The "Crisis" Plan:** What is our protocol if a major deliverable is at risk of being late? (e.g., An "All-Hands" emergency meeting, re-delegation of tasks?)
